A study on the diagnosis of deteriorated structures using the unmanned flying device photography

The project period: 21/11/2019~20/04/2020 

The theme of the 4th industrial revolution emerged anew at the 2016 World Economic Forum.

Industry-specific usages such as AI (Artificial Intelligence), VR (Virtual Reality), Internet of Things (IoT), 3D printing and unmanned flight device (drone) vary worldwide. With the emergence of various new industries that incorporate ICT technologies amid the fast-changing trend of the era, civil engineering colleges around the world are now focusing on education and research in technological convergence, unlike in the past when they focused on basic theory-oriented education. 

This project aims to diagnose safety and propose smart management measures through damage filming shoots, such as concrete corrosion and surface cracks on aged structures using an unmanned flight device (drone), which is still less practical in Hanoi, Vietnam. 

The social infrastructure including road bridges, buildings, dams, and concrete water reservoirs can become weak due to corrosion of steel cables if maintenance is neglected, and cracks or decomposition between the concrete can also occur due to the effects of climate such as typhoons and heavy rain. 

In the case of Vietnam, rapid economic development has led to an increase in investment in the construction sector, such as roads and civil engineering annually which further widens the scope of the construction sector. However, the safety management system for the continued maintenance of buildings in the public sector, which is directly linked to the safety of the people, has yet to be properly established, making management assessment standards or methods unsystematic and unreliable for results.

In light of this reality in Vietnam, the project aims to study and seek to deal with smart management measures by directly taking old structures photos and diagnosing stability based on the results obtained by using the newly emerging technology ‘drone(fly-cam)’ with the 4th industrial revolution. This project was performed by being supported from NRF (National Research Foundation of Korea) with two Vietnamese researchers over the period of about five months.
